Types of angles

Full turn

There are 360° in a full turn. If something rotates through a full turn, then you turn once all the way round, meaning you end up pointing the same way as you started.

These are examples of full turns. They're all the same angle, just pointing in different directions.

Straight line

There are 180° in a straight line. Straight lines are half of a full turn.

These are examples of straight lines. They're all the same angle, just pointing in different directions.

Right angle

There are 90° in a right angle. We normally mark right angles with a small square instead of part of a circle.

These are examples of right angles. They're all the same angle, just pointing in different directions.

One way of spotting right angles is that if one of the lines is horizontal (going straight from left to right), the other will be vertical (going straight up and down), just like in the first example above. In the other examples, if you tilt your head until one of the lines is horizontal, the other line should be vertical.
